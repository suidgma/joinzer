# Organizer Call Script — Joinzer

> First-organizer discovery + pilot calls. Goal: **even split** — genuinely learn how
> they run events AND convert good-fit organizers into a first pilot.
> Pilot market: Las Vegas metro (Henderson, Summerlin, Green Valley, North Las Vegas).
> Owner: Marty. Last revised: June 3, 2026.

---

## Why this call matters

Per `/CLAUDE.md`, the organizer conversation is the **#1 blocker**. It unblocks three things:
1. **Path A vs. B** — do organizers think of leagues and tournaments as one system or two?
2. **Pricing** — flat fee? per-registration? % of payments? free + paid payouts?
3. **Onboarding** — what's the real path from "interested" to "running their first event"?

So this is not a pitch. It's a structured conversation that *happens* to demo the product. If you
talk less than the organizer for the first 15 minutes, you're doing it right.

---

## Pre-call prep (5 min, before every call)

- [ ] Look them up: what events do they run? League, tournament, open play, clinics? How often?
- [ ] Find one specific thing about their operation to reference ("I saw you run the Thursday
      night mixed league at Henderson…").
- [ ] Know their likely current stack so you can ask about it specifically (TeamReach,
      GroupMe/WhatsApp, Pickleballbrackets.com, Google Forms + Venmo, DUPR, spreadsheets).
- [ ] Have the app open and logged in as an organizer, on a tournament with real-looking data,
      sitting on the **check-in / Players view** ready to share.
- [ ] Have the post-call capture sheet (bottom of this doc) open in another window.

---

## Framing the call (say this in the first 60 seconds)

> "Thanks for making time. Quick context: I'm building Joinzer, a platform for running
> pickleball leagues and tournaments here in Vegas. I'm talking to a handful of organizers
> *before* I lock down the next phase — I'm genuinely more interested in how you run your
> events today than in pitching you. If something I'm building is useful to you, great, we can
> talk about that at the end. Sound okay if I ask you a bunch of questions first?"

This earns honest answers, lowers their guard, and gives you permission to dig.

---

## Section 1 — Open & rapport (2 min)

- "How long have you been running events, and what got you into it?"
- "What are you running right now — leagues, tournaments, open play, a mix?"
- The specific reference from your prep.

Goal: get them talking, confirm the scale you're dealing with.

---

## Section 2 — Discovery (12–15 min)

Ask, then shut up. Follow the energy — when they get animated about a pain, dig there.

### A. How they run events today
- "Walk me through your last event from the moment you decided to run it to the day it happened."
- "How do players find out about it and sign up?"
- "How do you collect money — and when do *you* actually get paid?"
- "How do you build the schedule or bracket?"
- "On the day, how do you handle check-in, court assignments, and scores?"

### B. Tools & stack
- "What tools or apps are you using to pull this off right now?"
- "What do you pay for today — software, payment fees, anything?"
- "If you could delete one tool from your life tomorrow, which one and why?"

### C. The pains (where the gold is)
- "What's the part of running an event you dread most?"
- "When something goes wrong on event day, what is it usually?"
- "How much of your week does the admin eat up?"
- "Subs, waitlists, last-minute dropouts — how do you deal with those today?"
- "How do you communicate with players before and during an event?"

### D. Money & motivation
- "Are events a business for you, a side thing, or a labor of love?"
- "Roughly how many players across everything you run in a season?"
- "What would make a tool *worth paying for* to you — what would it have to save you?"

### E. Mental model (resolves Path A vs. B — ask naturally)
- "In your head, are leagues and tournaments basically the same job with different rules,
  or two completely different beasts to run?"

> **Listen for:** Do they describe one workflow or two? Do they reuse the same player roster
> across event types? This is the single most important answer for the schema decision.

---

## Section 3 — Targeted demo (6–8 min)

Do NOT tour the whole app. Show only what maps to the 2–3 pains they named. Bridge with:

> "You mentioned [their exact pain]. Let me show you how that works in Joinzer."

Pain → what to show (all of these are shipped):

| They complained about… | Show this |
|---|---|
| Chasing payments / getting paid late | Stripe Checkout registration + **Connect payout to the organizer's own account**; refunds; discount codes |
| Day-of chaos, check-in lines | **Check-in view (incl. QR)**, live court/match status (Playing / On Deck / Done) |
| Building brackets/schedules by hand | Divisions, bracket generation, live scoring |
| Subs & waitlists | Substitute pool + sub-request flow (leagues), waitlist with auto-promote (tournaments) |
| Group-chat sprawl | League group chat, in-app notifications, transactional email, organizer broadcast |
| Partner coordination | Solo + team registration, partner invites |
| Roster admin | CSV team import, co-organizer + volunteer roles |

Keep it to their reality. Every extra feature you show that they didn't ask about dilutes the
ones they did.

End the demo with a question, not a flourish:
> "Be honest — does any of that actually solve a problem you have, or is it solving a problem
> you don't really have?"

---

## Section 4 — Pricing probe & pilot ask (5 min)

### Pricing probe (do this before naming any number)
- "If this replaced [tools they named], what would feel fair to pay for it?"
- "Would you rather pay a flat fee, a small cut of registrations, or per-player — and why?"
- "Where does the money come from in your head — your pocket, or built into player fees?"

> Capture their framing verbatim. You're not negotiating; you're learning the pricing model.

### Pilot ask (the conversion half of the call)
> "Here's where I'm at: I want a small number of organizers to run a real event on Joinzer so
> I can make it exactly right for people like you. Do you have an event coming up in the next
> month or two that we could run on it together? I'll be hands-on the whole way."

- If yes → get the **specific event and date** before you hang up. That's the win.
- If hesitant → "What would have to be true for you to feel comfortable trying it on one event?"
- If no → "No problem — can I come back to you when [their blocker] is handled?"

---

## Section 5 — Close & next steps (2 min)

- Recap the 1–2 pains you heard most clearly ("So the biggest things are X and Y — did I get that right?").
- Confirm the concrete next step (pilot event + date, or a follow-up date).
- "Anyone else running events around town I should be talking to?" (referrals).
- Thank them. Send a short recap email same day.

---

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I already use [tool] and it's fine." | "Totally — what's the one thing it *doesn't* do well?" Then dig there, don't argue. |
| "My players won't download another app." | "There's no download — it's a web app, they tap a link. Want to see the player side?" |
| "I don't want to deal with switching mid-season." | "Makes sense. Could we line it up for your next event start instead?" |
| "How much does it cost?" (early) | "Honestly I'm still figuring that out with organizers like you — that's part of why we're talking. What would feel fair to you?" |
| "Is my players' data / money safe?" | "Payments run through Stripe directly to your account — I never touch the money. Player data is locked down per-user." |
| "I'm not technical." | "That's the point — if it's not faster than what you do now, I've failed. I'll set up your first event with you." |

## After 3–5 calls: synthesize

Pull the capture sheets together and answer, in `/CLAUDE.md` Open Decisions:
- **Path A vs. B** — did organizers describe one system or two?
- **Pricing** — what model and number kept coming up?
- **Onboarding** — what's the actual first-event path you need to build?
- **First committed event** — who, and what date?

The calls only count if they move these from "open" to "decided."
