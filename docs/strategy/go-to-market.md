# Joinzer — Go-to-Market

_Last updated: July 17, 2026_

> ⚠️ This is the least-proven doc and the most important one to act on. Joinzer's **#1 blocker** is that no organizer has seen the product and no first event is committed. Everything below is a **🟡 hypothesis** until that first organizer conversation happens. The plan's real purpose is to make that conversation happen and to learn from it.

## The beachhead

**Las Vegas metro** — Henderson, Summerlin, Green Valley, North Las Vegas. Saturate one dense, year-round market before expanding. A two-sided marketplace only works once local supply and demand are both present, so geographic focus is non-negotiable early.

## The motion: organizer-led (🟡)

Supply is the hard side. The sequence:

1. **Land one credible organizer** who already runs a real league or tournament.
2. **Run their next event on Joinzer** — ideally concierge / white-glove, so they take on zero setup risk.
3. **Their players onboard for free** to play in that event, getting a profile + rating + history.
4. **Players stick** via the directory/résumé/rating and start finding *other* games.
5. **Adjacent organizers notice** (their players are already on Joinzer) and are easier to land.
6. **Repeat until the metro has density**, then move to the next.

Why organizer-led and not player-led: a player-first launch fights cold-start (no games to find). An organizer brings a whole roster and a real event on day one.

## The first-organizer plan (the critical path)

This is the thing that unblocks the entire company. Concretely:

- **Identify** 5–10 real Las Vegas organizers (league runners, tournament directors, active rec-center/club coordinators). Prioritize by how much manual pain they clearly have and how many players they touch.
- **Offer a concierge first event:** Joinzer (Marty) helps set it up and runs it *with* them, free of platform fees for the pilot. Remove every reason to say no.
- **Success criteria for the pilot:** the event runs end-to-end on Joinzer; their players have active profiles; the organizer says, unprompted, that they'd do it again — and ideally, that they'd pay for it.
- **Learn:** use the conversation to answer the validation questions in `customers-and-personas.md` and to resolve the open decisions in `open-decisions.md` (Path A/B, pricing, beachhead persona).

Everything else in GTM is downstream of getting this one thing right.

## Player acquisition (downstream, 🟡)

Players mostly arrive *through* organizers, plus:

- **Public browse pages** (`/browse/leagues`, `/browse/tournaments`) — discoverable without an account.
- **Per-court SEO pages** (not built yet; ~65 courts in the DB) — a future organic-discovery channel.
- **Referrals** — players inviting partners/opponents into events they're already in.

Don't invest heavily in player-side acquisition until organizer supply exists to receive them.

## Growth loops (🔵 grounded in the product)

- **Rating / résumé loop:** players earn a Joinzer Score + build a match history → they stay to protect/grow it → retained players are inventory for the next organizer.
- **Organizer payout loop:** organizers get paid cleanly via Connect → they run more events → more players.
- **Directory loop:** more players in a metro → better discovery → more reason for new players and organizers to join.

## Channels to test (🟡)

Direct outreach to organizers; showing up at local courts/clubs; local Facebook pickleball groups; organizer referrals ("who else runs leagues around here?"). Rank by cost of Marty's time vs. quality of organizer reached — early on, high-touch/direct beats broad/cheap.

## Pilot metrics (🟡 — what to watch)

- Organizers contacted → conversations booked → events committed (the top of the funnel that matters most right now).
- First event: registrations, completion, no-show/sub rate handled in-app, payment volume.
- Player activation: % of an organizer's roster that creates a profile; % that return to find a second game.
- Organizer verdict: would they run the next one on Joinzer, paid?

## What unblocks GTM

A single sentence: **book and run the first organizer conversation.** It resolves the beachhead persona, informs pricing, decides Path A vs. B, and turns every hypothesis in this folder into something real. Until then, the product is ready and the market is untested.
