# Joinzer — Market & Competitive Landscape

_Last updated: July 17, 2026_

> Confidence note: competitor descriptions below draw on general knowledge of the pickleball-tech space (🟡 hypothesis) and need Marty's local, first-hand validation — feature sets and positioning change fast. The differentiation thesis is 🔵 grounded in what Joinzer actually ships.

## Market context (🟡)

Pickleball has been one of the fastest-growing participation sports, and organized play (leagues, ladders, tournaments, clubs) has grown alongside it. Rating has consolidated around **DUPR**, but the tooling to *run* organized play remains fragmented. That fragmentation is the opportunity.

## Why Las Vegas (🟡)

The pilot beachhead is the **Las Vegas metro** (Henderson, Summerlin, Green Valley, North Las Vegas): a dense, year-round-playable market where a single metro can be saturated to trigger local network effects before expanding. Concentrating supply (organizers) and demand (players) in one metro is how a two-sided marketplace escapes cold-start.

## The real competitor: the status quo

The thing to beat is **not** another app — it's the **stack of general-purpose tools** organizers already use:

- **Group texts / Facebook Groups** — coordination and announcements.
- **Spreadsheets** — rosters, schedules, standings.
- **Venmo / cash** — payments.
- **DUPR** — rating.
- **A bracket/registration site** — for tournaments.

It's free, familiar, and "good enough." Any pitch has to beat *inertia*, not just feature-match a rival.

## Named players (🟡 — validate locally)

| Player | What it does | Where it's strong | Where Joinzer differs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CourtReserve** | Club/facility management + court reservations, memberships, some events | Facilities and clubs with courts to manage | Joinzer isn't a facility/reservation system; it's organizer + player play-coordination, not court-booking |

| **TeamReach / Band / GroupMe** | Generic team communication | Familiar, free | Purpose-built for pickleball play, not generic chat |

## Joinzer's differentiation thesis (🔵 grounded)

1. **Four surfaces, one identity.** Coordination + leagues + tournaments + player directory share one auth, one profile, one rating. No competitor spans all four with a single player identity.
2. **Depth of organized-play features already shipped.** Five league formats; full tournament engine (brackets, live scoring, check-in incl. QR, reschedule, waitlist, schedule builder, offline run mode); a complete substitution system; player-run leagues.
3. **Payments + payouts built in.** Stripe Checkout + Connect Express means organizers can charge and *get paid*, with refunds and multi-division carts — not a bolt-on.
4. **A rating + résumé spine.** Joinzer Score/Level, match history, and a public player résumé are the player-retention layer that pure organizer tools lack.
5. **Mobile-first day-of + offline.** Built for the reality of running play on a phone at a court with bad signal.

## Risks & threats (❓)

- **Incumbent distribution.** DUPR or a bracket incumbent could add "run your league" features and reach organizers faster than Joinzer can.
- **DUPR owns identity.** If players anchor entirely on DUPR, Joinzer's own rating may struggle to matter.
- **Cold start.** Marketplace network effects don't exist until a metro is saturated; the first organizer is everything.
- **Breadth vs. focus.** Four surfaces built solo is a strength for the vision and a risk for depth/quality per surface.
