# Joinzer — Brand & Voice

_Last updated: July 17, 2026_

> Confidence: the product-facing lines already in the app (e.g., the hero tagline) are 🔵 grounded. The voice principles and attributes below are a **🟡 starting point** — a proposed source of truth to make generated copy consistent, meant to be edited as the brand sharpens.
>
> **Scope note:** this is **Joinzer's product brand voice** — how the app and its marketing talk to players and organizers. It is *not* Marty's personal thought-leadership voice (AI / human-evolution / execution), which is a separate persona. Don't conflate the two.

## What Joinzer is, in a sentence

The app a pickleball community runs its life on — find a game, run a season, play a tournament, know where you stand.

## Positioning lines (🔵 / 🟡)

- **Hero (in product):** "Play. Compete. Find your game."
- **Elevator:** "Joinzer brings finding games, running leagues and tournaments, payments, and ratings into one app — so organizers do less busywork and players just show up and play."
- **For organizers:** "Run your league or tournament without the spreadsheet-and-group-text circus. Take payments, get paid, and look pro."
- **For players:** "Find your next game, at your level, near you — and a rating that actually follows you."

## Brand attributes (🟡)

The feeling every touchpoint should leave:

- **Player-friendly, not corporate.** Written by someone who plays, for people who play.
- **Energetic but calm.** Enthusiastic about the game; reassuring about the logistics.
- **Local and real.** It's about *your* courts, *your* league, *your* people — not an abstract "platform."
- **Competent under pressure.** On tournament day, the tone is clear, fast, and confidence-inspiring — never cute when someone's trying to run an event.
- **Fair and transparent.** Especially around ratings, standings, subs, and money.

## Voice principles (🟡)

- **Speak to the person, not the feature.** "Are you playing today?" beats "Update attendance status."
- **Short, active, confident.** Prefer verbs. Cut hedging.
- **Plain over jargon** — except real pickleball terms players use (DUPR, doubles, dinking); use those naturally, they signal you're one of them. Explain Joinzer's own terms (Score vs. Level, box, ladder, flex) on first contact.
- **Urgency where it's real, calm everywhere else.** A payment due or a session tonight can be direct; a profile nudge shouldn't shout.
- **Encouraging, never condescending.** Celebrate a win, a streak, a first event — lightly.
- **US English**, throughout.

## Tone by surface (🟡)

- **Play / Coordination (player-first):** casual, quick, inviting. "Find a game." "You're in."
- **Leagues & Tournaments — day-of (organizer-first):** crisp, operational, dependable. Minimal words, maximal clarity.
- **Leagues & Tournaments — setup (desktop):** helpful and guiding; it's okay to be a bit more explanatory here.
- **Ratings & standings:** neutral, precise, fair — this is where trust is won or lost.
- **Money:** transparent and matter-of-fact. Never coy about fees or refunds.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "You're playing today 🎾" | "Attendance confirmed for session #14" |
| "Run tonight's session" | "Access the live session management interface" |
| "Yes, I'll be there" | "Submit RSVP" |
| Use one emoji as a friendly accent (🎾, 🏆) | Emoji-spam every line |
| "Finish setting up your tournament" | "Your tournament is in an incomplete state" |
| Name the league/court/person | Talk about "the entity" or "the resource" |

## Naming & terminology consistency

- Capitalize the product surfaces as proper features when referring to them as such: **Play, Leagues, Tournaments, Players.**
- **Joinzer Score** (the 0–100 number) and **Joinzer Level** (the label) are distinct — never use them interchangeably. (See `glossary.md`.)
- "Organizer," "captain," "host," "sub" have specific meanings — use them precisely (see `glossary.md`).

## Open brand questions (❓)

- Is "Joinzer" pronounced/positioned in a way that needs a tagline explainer for newcomers?
- Does the brand lean more "competitive/serious player" or "social/everyone"? The product serves both; the marketing may need to pick a lead.
