# Joinzer — Customers & Personas

_Last updated: July 17, 2026_

> ⚠️ **This is the most important doc to treat with humility.** No organizer or player has used Joinzer yet. Every persona below is a **🟡 hypothesis** built from the product's design and general knowledge of the pickleball scene — not from interviews. The value of this doc right now is less "here's who our customer is" and more "here's what we believe and exactly what to ask to find out." Update it the moment real input arrives.

## The two-sided shape

Joinzer is a marketplace: **organizers bring the players; players create the demand that makes organizing worth it.** Supply (organizers) is the hard side and the beachhead. Get one organizer, and you get their whole roster for free. This is why nearly all early effort points at organizers.

## Persona 1 — The Organizer (the beachhead) 🟡

**Who:** someone who already runs recurring pickleball for a community — a league director, a club/rec-center coordinator, or a tournament director. Often a passionate player who "fell into" organizing. May run one league or a whole local circuit.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Fill and manage a roster; handle no-shows and substitutes.
- Schedule sessions/rounds and keep scores/standings.
- Collect money and (increasingly) pay it back out.
- Look professional and reliable so players keep coming back.

**Pains today (assumed):** juggling a spreadsheet + a group chat + Venmo + a bracket site; chasing subs by text; manual standings; no clean way to take payment and pay themselves; everything breaks on tournament day.

**What would make them switch:** it has to make the *next* event materially easier than their current stack — ideally with a concierge first run so they feel zero setup risk. Organizers are busy and burned by half-baked tools; the bar is "obviously less work," not "more features."

**Willingness to pay (❓ open):** plausibly yes on a per-event transaction basis if Joinzer handles registration + payment + payouts; unclear whether they'll pay a subscription. Unknown who they'll want to bear the fee (themselves vs. players).

**Open sub-question — which organizer archetype is the true beachhead?** Independent league runner? Tournament director? Club/rec-center coordinator? These have different needs, price sensitivity, and volume. **We don't know yet, and it changes the roadmap.** (See `open-decisions.md`.)

## Persona 2 — The Captain 🟡

**Who:** a team or group leader inside a league — one level down from the organizer. The product increasingly empowers them (lineups, roster self-management, self-scoring, player-run sessions).

**JTBD:** field a lineup, keep their people informed, report scores, arrange subs without bothering the organizer.

**Why they matter:** captains reduce organizer load and deepen engagement. They're a retention lever, not an acquisition one — you reach them *through* the organizer.

## Persona 3 — The Player 🟡

Spans a spectrum:

- **Casual/social player** — wants to find a game near them tonight, at their level, without joining anything formal. Cares about ease and belonging.
- **Competitive/league player** — wants standings, a rating that means something, the right partner/opponent, and a record of how they're doing.

**JTBD:** find a game/partner/opponent; know my level and see it improve; show up and play with minimal friction; not get stuck in a game that's too easy or too hard.

**Pains today (assumed):** doesn't know where the games are; skill mismatches; rating lives in a separate silo (DUPR); coordination happens in chats they're not in.

**Willingness to pay (❓ open):** likely resistant to paying to *find* games; may pay to *enter* paid leagues/tournaments (which they already do today). The player-side product is probably free, monetized through the paid events they join.

**Retention hook (🔵 grounded):** the profile/résumé + Joinzer Score/Level + match history is designed to be the reason a player stays after their organizer's season ends.

## Persona 4 — The Facility / Club (later) 🟡

Rec centers, clubs, and courts could be an organizer *type* or a distribution channel, but there is **no organizations/business layer yet** — every event today is owned by a single individual. Treat facilities as a future expansion, not an early target.

## Questions to validate in the first organizer conversation

This is the concrete payoff of this doc. Before assuming any of the above, get answers to:

1. Walk me through how you run your league/tournament today, tool by tool. Where does it hurt most?
2. How do you handle **money** — registration, refunds, paying yourself? What does that cost you in fees and time?
3. How do you deal with **no-shows and substitutes** today?
4. What would have to be true for you to run your **next** event on a new tool? What's the risk you're worried about?
5. Who should pay Joinzer's fee — you or your players? What fee would feel fair?
6. What do your **players** complain about?
7. Would you pay a monthly fee, or only a cut of paid events?
8. What's the one thing that, if it broke on event day, would make you never use it again?

The answers reshuffle this entire doc — and the roadmap. Until then, hold these personas loosely.
