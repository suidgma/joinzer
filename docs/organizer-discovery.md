# Organizer Discovery Guide

> A field guide for the first real conversations with pickleball organizers.
> **Goal: learn, not pitch.** Per `/CLAUDE.md`, no organizer has used Joinzer yet — this
> conversation is the #1 blocker. It unblocks three decisions we cannot make from the
> code: **Path A vs. B (schema)**, **pricing**, and **onboarding design**.
>
> Last revised: July 8, 2026.

---

## How to use this

- One organizer at a time, 20–30 min. Record it (with permission) or take notes against the
  capture template at the bottom.
- **Talk about their world, not our product.** The best answers are about what already
  happened, not what they think they'd do. Save any demo for the *end* (or a second call).
- **Listen ~80% of the time.** Follow the pain. When they mention a workaround or a
  frustration, dig — that's the gold.
- Don't ask "would you use / pay for X?" (people are polite and wrong). Ask what they do and
  pay for **today**.

---

## Part 1 — Context (who they are, what they run)

Quick, factual — sets the frame.

- What do you organize — leagues, tournaments, open play, or a mix? What sport(s)?
- How big? How often? How long have you been doing it?
- Do you run it solo, or with co-organizers / volunteers?

## Part 2 — Current workflow & pain *(the most important section)*

- **"Walk me through the last event you ran, start to finish."** Let them tell the whole
  story. Note every tool and every manual step.
- What tools do you use today? (Spreadsheets, GroupMe/TeamReach/band, paper, DUPR, another
  app, DMs…) For *what*, specifically?
- Where does it eat your time? What's the worst part of running an event?
- Tell me about the last time something went wrong — registration, scheduling, scores,
  no-shows, payments. What happened?
- What have you already tried to fix that? Did you pay for anything, or build your own hack?

> Listen for: the ONE pain big enough that they'd switch tools to solve it. That's the wedge.

## Part 3 — Money *(pricing signal — behavioral, not hypothetical)*

- Do you charge players? How much, and how do you collect it today (Venmo, cash, app…)?
- Do you pay for any tools/software now? Which, and roughly how much?
- When money moves for an event, whose problem is it — yours or the players'?

> Don't ask "would you pay for Joinzer?" Infer willingness-to-pay from what they already pay
> for and how much pain the money-handling causes.

## Part 4 — Leagues vs. tournaments *(unblocks Path A vs. B)*

- Do you run **both** leagues and tournaments, or mostly one?
- In your head, are those the same kind of thing or totally different?
- If you use software, is it the same tool for both, or different tools?

> Signal: if organizers treat them as one workflow, unifying under `competitions` (Path B)
> has user value. If they're mentally/operationally separate, keeping them separate (Path A)
> is fine — don't pay migration cost for a distinction users don't feel.

## Part 5 — Adoption & onboarding *(unblocks onboarding design)*

- If you tried a new tool for your next event, what would make you give up in the first 10
  minutes?
- Who else would have to touch it — co-organizers, players — and how tech-comfortable are they?
- *(Near the end, optional):* "Mind if I show you where you'd start?" — then **watch them
  try to create their first event and say nothing.** Note exactly where they hesitate or ask
  "what do I do now?" That's your onboarding backlog, written by a real user.

## Part 6 — Close (commitment + referrals)

- **"Would you be up for running your next real event on it?"** — the real test of interest
  is a commitment, not a compliment.
- Who else runs events around here that I should talk to?
- Can I follow up when it's ready for you?

## After ~3–5 conversations

Look for the pattern, not the outlier:
- Is there **one pain** every organizer named? → that's the thing to make excellent.
- Did anyone treat leagues+tournaments as one workflow? → weigh Path B; otherwise stay Path A.
- What do they already pay for? → anchors the pricing model (per-event fee, % of registration, flat subscription, free-for-organizer/players-pay).
- Where did every organizer stall on the demo? → next onboarding work (Phase 3+), now written by users instead of guessed.

Then update `/CLAUDE.md` §6 Open Decisions with what the conversations resolved.
